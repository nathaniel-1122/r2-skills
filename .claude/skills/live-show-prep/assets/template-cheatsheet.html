Fiber-optic & interceptor drones

Fiber-optic drone: thin glass cable trails behind — no radio, nothing to jam. 5–20 km (up to 50). Cable NOT reeled in — single-use, abandoned on impact. Answer to Russian jamming.

Interceptor drones: $2.5–5k; kill slow Shaheds by ramming. Cannot touch a ballistic missile (Mach 5–12, steep arc) — that still needs a $4M Patriot. Future = layered, cost-tiered defense.

Russia's air war & recent strikes

"Combined strike": cheap drones first (≈half are warhead-less decoys) to make Ukraine spend → ballistic/cruise missiles into the gaps. Magazine-depletion by design.

HIMARS: still firing, but Russia jammed the GPS & moved targets past ~80 km. Story moved to ATACMS (300 km) & new PrSM (400+ km, 2x per pod).

Why Hormuz = leverage

Geography > missiles. Iran sits on the one chokepoint the world economy can't route around. Economy smaller than Florida's, but it can move U.S. gas prices.

The paradox: full closure chokes Iran's own exports to China (its lifeline + protector). So Iran wants calibrated disruption — scary, not shut. Selective "friendly-nation" passage + tolls is that calibration.

Coercion without closure

Toolkit: GPS jamming/spoofing · fast-boat swarms · mines (the threat alone freezes insurers) · coastal drones/missiles · ship seizures.

Seize > sink: a hostage is a bargaining chip; a wreck is a war. Seizing panics insurers without the death toll that forces a U.S. response.

Logic: don't sink half the tankers — just make the route expensive, unpredictable, politically dangerous. Insurance does the rest.

New battlefield — quick hits

Phone data: CENTCOM confirmed (May '26) adversaries bought troops' commercial location data to target/surveil them. No hack. Advertising ID still not disabled on issued phones. (CENTCOM didn't name Iran.)

AI war room: Pentagon elevating Palantir's Maven inside CJADC2; commercial AI (Anthropic/OpenAI/Google/xAI) used in Iran war. Decision-support, not autonomous. Anthropic–DoD court fight = the dependency tension.

Drug-boat war: Op. Southern Spear — 200+ killed, 60+ strikes since fall '25. Legality + effectiveness contested; boats mostly carry cocaine (to Europe), not U.S. fentanyl (which comes overland via Mexico).

Viewer Q&A — lightning answers

Fiber-optic cable reeled back in? No — one-way, disposable; destroyed on impact.
Doesn't Europe have Patriots? Yes, but stocks are thin & the scarce missiles come off the same U.S. line. Redistributes scarcity.
Why no laws to fix production? There are — 7-yr deal to triple PAC-3 + $4.76B contract. Catch: factories take till ~2028–2030.
Why factories in neighborhoods? Soviet "monotown" plant-plus-housing planning + modern dispersal. Frozen geography, 70–80 yrs old.
Interceptor-drone math? Works on slow drones; can't touch ballistic missiles — those still need Patriots.
"Occupied territory"? Held by force, claimed on paper, recognized by almost no one — Donetsk, Luhansk, Zaporizhzhia, Kherson + Crimea. UN voted 143–5.
Where's your data from? Lockheed filings, CSIS, U.S. Army, Ukrainian Air Force Cmd, EIA. Ranges where classified.
What happened to HIMARS? Countered — GPS jamming + targets moved past ~80 km. Now one tool among many; ATACMS/PrSM fly farther.
Taiwan points U.S. missiles at China? Real — HIMARS, ATACMS, Harpoon, Patriot delivering now ($11B Dec '25 deal). Defensive deterrence; Ukraine is the live lesson.
U.S. shortage hits Japan? Japan just exported PAC-3 to backfill the U.S.; its own output capped by a shared Boeing-seeker bottleneck.
